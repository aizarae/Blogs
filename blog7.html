Crypto Day

Leap Day: Aug. 13, 2021

In recent years, as I entered my twenties, it has suddenly become apparent to me that I would have to think of my foreseeable future and become financially independent. Since I have always only relied on allowances to save money, even less so in the current situation where my allowance has significantly decreased and therefore hindered my plans of growing money faster, I have been recommended to look into cryptocurrencies and finally join the game. So as part of the mandatory attendance in LEAP classes, I chose to attend a class called Crypto Day organized and facilitated by the Investorsâ€™ Society. Even though I have done some self-research on cryptos and what they entail before, I knew I still had a lot to learn in terms of understanding them on a deeper level.

Crypto Day dissected the fundamentals that allowed both beginners and intermediates in cryptocurrency to appreciate its pivotal surge. It tackled the history, inception, and important terms that are typically used when discussing and understanding cryptocurrency. In the continuous progression of society into a more technology-based environment, it is no surprise that crypto has exponentially grown its impact for both investors and netizens alike. Although cryptocurrency also has received its own sets of flak due to its volatility and seemingly fraudulent existence, being immersed in something intangible yet possessing a significant impact on both innovation and economy makes me feel like I belong to that community that provides this.

Having a realization that sooner or later I would have to provide for myself and stand on my own feet opened me to the possibility of investing in cryptocurrency. Being surrounded by people who also believe in its potential and those who know how to manipulate it to their advantage has also given me new knowledge and insights on the crypto world. By attending Crypto Day, I was able to appreciate the steps that modernization brings.
